World Bank · Africa Gender Innovation Lab

Ethiopia Gender Scorecard: From Research to National Policy

Situation

Financial institutions in Ethiopia lacked a standardized way to assess how well they were serving women — leaving a critical gap in gender-responsive financial inclusion strategy at the national level.

Task

Co-lead design and implementation of a first-of-its-kind institutional assessment tool, in partnership with the National Bank of Ethiopia.

Action

Built the Gender Scorecard framework covering institutional practices, product design, and customer outcomes. Coordinated data collection across financial institutions and synthesized findings for policymakers.

Result

Ethiopia became only the second country globally to adopt such a framework. Findings were incorporated into Ethiopia's National Financial Inclusion Strategy and shaped central bank policy targets for women's financial access.

Give Girls A Chance · Nigeria

Co-Founding a National Literacy Ecosystem for Girls

Situation

Girls in underserved Nigerian communities — including refugees — faced compounding barriers to education, with programs addressing classrooms without engaging families or communities.

Task

Co-found an organization capable of building durable literacy improvement through sustained ecosystem engagement, not one-off interventions.

Action

Designed STEAM, mentorship, and menstrual hygiene programs alongside a whole-child literacy model. Launched and ran an annual national essay competition. Drove fundraising and secured partnerships. Provided scholarships and mentoring to 350+ girls.

Result

$3M+ mobilized in philanthropic funding. 1,000+ entries in the 2025 essay competition from 150+ schools across 20 states. Measurable performance gains among enrolled girls. Commonwealth Points of Light Award 2022.

Civil Society Work

Give Girls A Chance

Give Girls A Chance (GGAC) is a nonprofit I co-founded to advance literacy, education, and economic empowerment for girls and young women in underserved communities in Nigeria — including refugee women. It is the most personal expression of what I believe about how durable change happens.

GGAC runs a portfolio of programs: a STEAM initiative, a mentorship program, a Menstrual Hygiene Management Program, and an annual national essay competition now in its 8th year. These programs have provided scholarships and mentoring to more than 350 girls and young women. The 2025 essay competition received 1,000+ entries from 150+ schools across 20 states.

GGAC's work was recognized with the Commonwealth Points of Light Award in 2022 — awarded by the Commonwealth Secretariat for exceptional voluntary service.
